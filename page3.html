Sun Valley Mustard Journey

A Valley icon started with a dream and a garage......

It was the spring of 1984.

Armed with a 50 year-old recipe and her determination, Lois Allison, a.k.a. The Mustard Lady, founded the company in her single-car garage in 1984. 

First and most successful account. 

In the beginning, most of the ingredients were supplied by Atkinsons’ Market, my first and most successful account. Not only did they give me display space, the deli always offered Sun Valley Mustard on sandwich orders. (And they still do.) On Fridays, in the back of Atkinsons’, I did food demos dressed in seasonal costumes and lured hungry customers with crackers, a dab of mustard and a sliver of cheese.
("Lois Allison")

Changing Ownerships

Six years later, she sold the company she had named Sun Valley Mustard, and it’s since grown into a purveyor of world famous, award-winning, hand-crafted artisan mustards.

Ambitious Ownership

Company owner Joshua Wells brings a dizzyingly diverse background to the company.

A Bald-Faced Redesign

The company’s entire product line went through a packaging redesign. The Bald Mountain image is predominately displayed on every jar of mustard the company sells, with distinctly colored backgrounds to easily differentiate flavors on the store shelf. Customers love the new design, and it won the Gold Medal for Design at the 2012 World Wide Mustard Championships – despite a last minute entry.

A Mountain of Awards

Sun Valley Mustard’s growing list of awards and accolades includes the Silver Medal for their Spicy-Sweet flavor and a Gold Medal for their label & packaging design at the 2012 World-Wide Mustard Championships in Middleton, Wisconsin. Several Sun Valley Mustard favors have also won prizes at the Napa Valley Mustard Festival Worldwide Competition. There are also several first-place finishes in the Food Distribution Magazine National Mustard Competition, awarded for victory in a blind taste-test competition.

"Celebrating 30 years of mustardmaking"

In 2014, Sun Valley Mustard is celebrating 30 years of mustardmaking. “Sun Valley Mustard is really a local legend,” says owner-operator Josh Wells.

Plans for Growth

Company Owner Joshua said, “We are working on diversifying our product offerings – we will need assistance with our packaging. Be ready!”
